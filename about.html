Chandigarh, renowned for its modern urban design, is home to several esteemed educational institutions, each with its unique history and contributions to academia. Below is an overview of some prominent colleges in Chandigarh: Panjab University (PU): Established in 1882 in Lahore, Panjab University relocated to India after the partition in 1947, initially operating from Solan before moving to its current campus in Chandigarh in 1956. The university has a rich tradition of excellence in teaching and research across various disciplines. ​ Wikipedia +1 Panjab University Chandigarh +1 Panjab University Chandigarh Post Graduate Government College, Sector 11 (PGGC-11): Founded on May 26, 1953, PGGC-11 began its journey in Sector 10 before moving to its permanent location in Sector 11 in 1960. The college offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs and has contributed significantly to the region's educational landscape. ​ Wikipedia Chandigarh College of Architecture (CCA): Established on August 7, 1961, CCA was set up to impart education in architecture, aligning with Chandigarh's vision as a planned city. The college has played a pivotal role in shaping architectural education in India. ​ Wikipedia Punjab Engineering College (PEC): Originally founded in 1921 in Mughalpura, Lahore, as Mughalpura Technical College, PEC relocated to Roorkee post-partition and then to Chandigarh in 1953. Over the years, it has evolved into a premier institution for engineering education and research. ​ Wikipedia Chandigarh Group of Colleges (CGC): Established in 2001 at Landran and later expanded to Jhanjeri in 2012, CGC has grown to become a significant educational hub offering diverse programs in engineering, management, and other fields. ​ Wikipedia +1 Chandigarh Engineering College +1 Chandigarh University (CU): Founded on July 10, 2012, by an act of the Punjab State Legislature, CU has rapidly gained recognition for its emphasis on research, innovation, and interdisciplinary learning. ​
